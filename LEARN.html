BURNS

WHAT IS BURN?

tissue damage

REASON?

when exposed to sun, fire, electricity, chemicals

Major or minor burn?

Call 911 for major burns:

  • deep damage
  • dry skin
  • blackened or patches on skin
  • covers a major portion

Minor burn:

  • Superficial, not deep
  • Aches
  • Blisters
  • Small patch

For major burns

First Aid til help arrives:

  • Bring the patient to a safe position. In case of electrical burns, make sure that the power supplies have been turned off.
  • If patient is not breathing, start rescue breathing.
  • Burned area will swell. Remove any tight items such as belts, jwellery etc. quickly and gently
  • Cover it with moist cloth or bandage.
  • Don't immerse large severe burnt area into water which could lead to loss of body heat
  • Elevate the burned area above the heart.
  • Keep an eye for any signs of shock, fainting, paleness, shallow breathing

For minor burns

  • Apply cool compresses or hold the burned area under cool running tap
  • Remove any tight items like jewelry quickly and gently
  • Don't pop the blisters. If it pops, clean it and apply an antibiotic cream. If a rash appears, don't use the cream.
  • Apply moisturiser or aloe vera gel or any other lotion to cool the burned area which prevents it from dring and provides relieve
  • Use a sterile gauze bandage to cover the burned area lightly to avoid air exposure
  • If required, take a pain reliever (buprofen etc)

HEART ATTACK

Call 911

Symptoms:

  • Pain, tightness, or a feeling of suffocation in the center of the chest that spreads (shoulders, arms, back, neck area, jaw)
  • Nausea, indigestion, heartburn or abdominal pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizzy
  • Sweating

can last for more than 15 minutes

can be sudden or one may have signs for hours/days in advance

For heart attack:

  • Call 911 or drive to nearest hospital (the patient should drive only and only if no other option is available)
  • Chew an aspirin which reduces blood clotting(don't if had been advised otherwise/allergic)
  • Take nitroglycerin (if have been advised by doctor)
  • Begin CPR if the patient has stopped breathing

    • Push hard on the center of the chest rapidly and rhythmicly 100 to 120 times a minute.

  • Use an automated external defibrillator/AED (if available) following the device manual and the patient has fainted.

SNAKE BITE

Call 911:

  • venomous snake
  • difficulty in breathing
  • faints

Do:

1. Note the description of the snake so as to tell the emergency staff

2. Protect the patient

While waiting for medical help:

  • Bring the patient to a safe position
  • Lay down, wound below the heart
  • Keep calm and avoid any movementto to avoid venom from spreading
  • Clean the wound with soap and water and loosely tie with clean and dry sterile bandage
  • Remove any jewelry or any other tight items from the wound

Do not:

  • Cut the wound
  • Attempt to suck out venom
  • Apply tourniquet, ice, or water
  • Give alcohol or caffeinated drinks or any medications

Symptoms

  • Nonvenomous: pain, scratches
  • Venomous snake: severe burning pain (within 15-30 mins), swell, bruise, spreads, nausea, breathing problems, weakness, odd taste in the mouth
  • Others (eg. coral snakes): neurological symptoms (skin tingling, difficulty speaking)
  • Sometimes, a venomous snake can bite without injecting venom

FRACTURE

Don't move the person until and unless necessary

Call 911
Do:

  • Apply pressure to stop any bleeding using clean bandage or cloth
  • Don;t move(especially neck or back fractures) or try to reallign the bone of the injured area. If know how and emergency staff is taking time to arrive, to reduce discomfort, apply and pad splints above and below the fractured area
  • Use ice packs wrapped in clean cloth to reduce swelling and pain
  • Shock, faint, shortness of breath: lay head lower than the trunk, elevate the legs, keep them warm(use blanket etc)
  • Torn skin: cover with a sterile bandage/clean cloth

Cast care:

  • to reduce swelling, place cast on pillows, elevate it above the level of the heart(24-48 hrs)
  • Use cold compresses covered in clean cloth to the swollen area for 20 mins in every 2 hrs
  • Take painkillers (Acetaminophen/Ibuprofen) for at least 48 hrs
  • Keep the cast dry
  • If gets wet, dry it immediately : use a hairdryer on a cool setting (hot settings might burn your skin)
  • Keep the cast clean : to avoid infection
  • Never insert anyything into the cast if feels itchy : might damage the skin, infection

Visit a doctor if:

  • skin rash/inside of the cast smells
  • tight/loose cast, cast gets very wet
  • increased pain and swelling, difficulty in moving fingers
  • numbness in the arm/fingers/toes
  • fingers feel cold/turns blue

ELECTRIC SHOCK

Call 911

Symptoms

  • Severe burns
  • Confused
  • Breathing problems
  • Heart rhythm problems
  • Cardiac arrest
  • Muscle pain and contractions
  • Seizures
  • Faints

Do:

  • Turn off the source power supply/move away from the source, use a nonconducting item(cardboard/plastic/wooden)
  • Breathing stops, no movement : begin CPR
  • Prevent from being chilled
  • Cover any burned areas with a sterile gauze bandage/clean cloth

If high voltage lines are involved:

  • shut them off
  • if a tingling sensation in your legs/lower body, hop on one foot to a safe place and wait for power to shut off
  • Power line falls on a car : passengers should stay inside unless conditions for explosion/fire arises
